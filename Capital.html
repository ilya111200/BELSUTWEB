How works our deposit system

A deposit account is a bank account maintained by a financial istitution in which a customer can deposit and withdraw money. Deposit accounts can be saving accounts, current accounts or any of several other types of accounts explained below. Transactions on deposit accounts are recorded in a bank's books, and the resulting balance is recorded as a liability of the bank and represents an amount owed by the bank to the customer. In other words, the banker-customer (depositor) relationship is one of debtor-creditor. Some banks charge fees for transactions on a customer's account. Additionally, some banks pay customers interests on their account balances.
